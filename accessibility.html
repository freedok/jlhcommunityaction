Accessibility & Compliance Statement

Johnston-Lee-Harnett Community Action, Inc.

Johnston-Lee-Harnett Community Action, Inc. (JLHCA) is committed to ensuring that all individuals — including people with disabilities — have full and equal access to our programs, services, activities, facilities, and digital content. As a Community Action Agency receiving federal funding, we comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 (Sections 504 and 508), and follow CAPLAW guidance on nondiscrimination and equal access.

Our mission is rooted in equity, dignity, and inclusion. We work continuously to ensure that our physical spaces, communication methods, and online services are accessible to everyone.

Digital Accessibility Standards

JLHCA strives to ensure that jlhcommunityaction.org meets or exceeds:

  • WCAG 2.1 Level AA
  • WCAG 2.2 emerging standards
  • Section 508 accessibility requirements

To support an inclusive digital experience, we maintain:

  • Keyboard-accessible navigation
  • Text alternatives for images
  • Semantic HTML structure
  • WCAG-compliant color contrast
  • Accessible online forms with clear labels and error messages
  • Responsive design for all devices
  • Consistent navigation patterns

We regularly review our website to improve clarity, usability, and accessibility.

ADA Compliance & Equal Access

In accordance with the ADA and CAPLAW guidance, JLHCA ensures that no individual is excluded, denied services, or discriminated against on the basis of disability. We provide reasonable accommodations to ensure equal participation in all programs and activities.

Examples of accommodation include:

  • Alternative formats (large print, accessible PDFs, plain text)
  • Assistance completing applications or forms
  • Sign language interpreters or relay services
  • Language access services
  • Communication supports for individuals with hearing, vision, or cognitive disabilities
  • Website accessibility support

Accommodation is provided free of charge.

Section 508 Compliance

As a federally funded organization, JLHCA works to ensure that our electronic and information technology is accessible to individuals with disabilities. Our Section 508 efforts include:

  • Ensuring compatibility with assistive technologies
  • Remediating or replacing inaccessible PDFs and documents
  • Providing captions or transcripts for multimedia content
  • Ensuring keyboard-only access to all interactive components
  • Using ARIA attributes where appropriate
  • Maintaining accessible online forms for Crisis Assistance, Head Start, Weatherization, and other programs

Requesting Accommodations or Reporting Barriers

If you need accommodation, encounter an accessibility barrier, or require information in an alternative format, please contact our ADA Coordinator:

ADA Grievance Procedure

Individuals who believe they have been denied access or discriminated against based on disability may file a grievance with the ADA Coordinator. Complaints may be submitted verbally or in writing and will be addressed promptly, fairly, and in accordance with federal civil rights requirements.

Ongoing Commitment

Accessibility is an ongoing process. JLHCA is actively working to:

  • Expand accessible online forms, including Crisis Assistance and program applications
  • Improve document accessibility across all departments
  • Train staff on ADA, Section 508, and digital accessibility best practices
  • Integrate accessibility into future website updates and redesigns
