JLH Community Action Head Start / Early Head Start Program

This three-county, comprehensive child development program is designed to meet children and family needs thru licensed center-based care. The primary goal of the Head Start/Early Head Start programs is providing quality developmentally appropriate learning experiences for children birth to five years of age. Early Head Start/Head Start provides a comprehensive child development program that addresses the individual, physical, educational, nutritional, mental, social and emotional development of infants, toddlers and preschool participants. The program also serves the needs of pregnant mothers during prenatal and post-delivery.

Frequently Asked Questions

What do I need to bring to the first day of school?

A change of clothes.

Do I need to bring food?

Meals are served daily (breakfast, lunch, & snack).

Do I need to bring diapers / pull-ups?

No, they are provided.

Do I need to bring formula?

No, it is provided.

Can I volunteer?

Yes — you must be at least 18 years old. If you want to be a regular volunteer, you will need a TB test and a background check.

Can I bring snacks?

Yes. Please refer to our healthy snacks list (link coming soon).

Who should I contact to update information?

Your Family Advocate.

Can I donate?

Yes. Please refer to our in-kind donation page (link coming soon).

Why is transportation limited?

Due to outdated buses, the routes are limited.

Can my Early Head Start child ride the bus?

No. The Office of Head Start does not allow us to transport infants and toddlers.

My child has food allergies. Can I bring my child’s lunch?

No. However, please get in touch with our Nutrition Department for meal options.

What is an IFSP and what is an IEP?

IFSP — Individualized Family Service Plan: a written plan that guides services for a child younger than 3 and their family’s stated outcomes. An IFSP is written after the child is evaluated and found eligible for early intervention services by the public school.

IEP — Individualized Education Plan: a written plan developed by the public school to ensure that a child older than 3 with a disability identified under the law receives specialized instruction and related services in learning settings.

Will my child be labeled?

No, we do not put any labels on children. We understand that labeling can affect a child’s self-esteem, behavior, and how they are treated.

Will my child be able to receive services during the day at Early Head Start / Head Start?

Yes. Children and their families are entitled to receive any special education and related services identified in an IFSP or IEP, provided at no cost to the family, by private therapists.
